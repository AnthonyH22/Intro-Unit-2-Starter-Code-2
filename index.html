Monkeypox: What is this strange disease?

A rare condition known as monkeypox is brought on by infection with the monkeypox virus. The variola virus, which causes smallpox, and the monkeypox virus are both members of the same virus family. Smallpox symptoms are comparable to those of monkeypox, but they are milder, and monkeypox rarely results in death.


What is Monkeypox?

Monkeypox is a viral disease affecting rodents and primates in rainforest areas of west and central Africa,related to smallpox and sometimes transmitted to humans. Monkeypox is usually a self-limited disease with its symptoms lasting from 2 to 4 weeks. However like most diseases, severe cases can occur.

Below are the general symptoms of the Monkeypox virus:

  • Fever
  • Skin rash
  • Headache
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Tiredness
  • Muscle aches and backaches
  • Chills

Monkeypox has actually been discovered way back in the 1950's. About a few months ago, it's been discovered in Europe and North America.

How does this virus spread?

As research has shown, Monkeypox can be spread in several ways.

Monkeypox can spread to anyone through close/personal, often skin-to-skin contact, including but not limited to:
  • Hugging, massage, and kissing
  • Touching contaminated items of others who have contracted Monkeypox
  • Sexual activities

How can we protect ourselves?

Here are a few steps you can take to stay safe:
  • Wash your hands consistently
  • Avoid touching/sharing any contaminated objects
  • Avoid close skin to skin contact with those that has a rash similar to Monkeypox
  • Isolate yourself from those who have monkeypox/who were in close contact
Most importantly, just be cautious and careful at all times, you never know what could happen.

Monkeypox is still a relatively new resurfaced virus so be patient and stay updated with news. The CDC and WHO are consistently working and finding new information to let us know.
